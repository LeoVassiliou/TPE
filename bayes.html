How This Works — Bayesian Inference

1. Set Your Prior P(God)

This is your credence in God's existence before examining any argument. It encodes your background epistemic state. A neutral starting point is 50%. A committed atheist might set 5%; a committed theist might set 95%.

2. Rate Premises for Each Argument

Each argument has premises. Your slider value (0–100%) is your credence that the premise is true. Read the evidence and objections carefully before rating. Unrated premises are excluded from the calculation.

3. Premises Combine Into a Likelihood Ratio

Each argument produces a Likelihood Ratio (LR) — how much more likely is the argument's evidence given God versus given no God. Arguments FOR God produce LR > 1 (raise probability). Arguments AGAINST God produce LR < 1 (lower probability). The LR for each argument is computed from your premise ratings and a built-in base LR for that argument.

4. Bayes' Theorem Updates Your Posterior

Posterior odds = Prior odds × LR₁ × LR₂ × … × LRₙ
Posterior P = Posterior odds / (1 + Posterior odds)
This chains across all activated arguments simultaneously.

5. "Against God" Arguments Work the Same Way

The Problem of Evil, Divine Hiddenness, etc. each produce LR < 1 when you accept their premises — pushing your posterior down. The final posterior integrates evidence from both sides automatically.

Bayesian Formula:
• Prior odds = P(God) / (1 − P(God))
• Argument LR = base_LR ^ (avg_premise_confidence) — where base_LR encodes how philosophically strong the argument is if all premises hold at 100%
• Net LR = LR_for₁ × LR_for₂ × … × (1/LR_against₁) × …
• Posterior odds = prior_odds × net_LR
• Posterior P(God) = posterior_odds / (1 + posterior_odds)
• An LR = 1 means the argument is neutral — no update. LR = 10 means the evidence is 10× more likely given God.
📖 Glossary
Prior Probability
Your credence before examining arguments. The Bayesian starting point. Neither confirmed nor denied by the math — you set it honestly.
Likelihood Ratio (LR)
LR = P(evidence | God) / P(evidence | no God). LR > 1 supports God; LR < 1 undermines God; LR = 1 is neutral. Bayes multiplies your odds by the LR.
Posterior Probability
Your credence after updating on all the arguments you rated. This is the output of the Bayesian calculation.
Valid Syllogism
An argument where, if all premises are true, the conclusion must be true. Atheistic arguments here are formally valid — the only question is whether the premises are true.
S5 Modal Logic
If possibly necessary, then necessary (◇□P → □P). Used in the Ontological Argument.
BGV Theorem
Borde-Guth-Vilenkin (2003): any universe with average positive expansion must have a past spacetime boundary.
