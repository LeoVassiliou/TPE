How This Bayesian System Works

Set Your Prior

Start with your baseline belief in theism (default 50%). This represents your confidence before considering any specific arguments.

Rate Theistic Arguments

For each premise, rate your confidence (0-100%). The core conclusion equals the product of premise probabilities, then gets multiplied by your prior to update your belief.

Rate Atheistic Arguments

These work in reverse - strong atheistic arguments reduce your theism probability. Each atheistic conclusion is subtracted from theistic properties.

Bayesian Updates

Your posterior after each argument becomes the prior for the next. Evidence accumulates properly without double-counting through rigorous Bayesian conditioning.

Property Synthesis

Multiple arguments supporting the same property combine via noisy-OR. Atheistic arguments provide negative evidence that reduces property probabilities.

Final Worldview

The system computes your final theism probability accounting for all evidence. The analysis shows where you land on the belief spectrum.

Bayesian Mathematics:
• Prior: P(God) - your starting belief
• Likelihood: P(Evidence|God) / P(Evidence|¬God) - how much the evidence favors theism
• Posterior: P(God|Evidence) = Prior × Likelihood / Normalization
• Atheistic evidence: P(Property) × (1 - AtheisticStrength)
• Final synthesis: NoiseOR(PositiveEvidence) × Product(AtheisticDiscounts)
📖 Bayesian Glossary
Bayes' Theorem
P(H|E) = P(E|H)P(H)/P(E). The fundamental rule for updating beliefs in light of evidence. Posterior = Likelihood × Prior / Evidence probability.
Prior Probability
Your initial belief before considering specific evidence. Represents background knowledge, intuition, and default assumptions about the hypothesis.
Likelihood Ratio
P(E|H)/P(E|¬H). How much more probable the evidence is under the hypothesis than under its negation. Values >1 favor the hypothesis.
Posterior Probability
Your updated belief after considering the evidence. This becomes the new prior for evaluating additional evidence in sequence.
Noisy-OR
Probabilistic combination assuming independent evidence sources: P(effect) = 1 − ∏(1 − P(causeᵢ)). Prevents double-counting while aggregating evidence.
Evidence Strength
How decisively evidence bears on the hypothesis. Strong evidence has high likelihood ratios; weak evidence has ratios near 1.0.
